Understanding Digital Resilience

In today’s world, digital technologies are present in most areas of life. People socialize, explore, create and work in digital environments. Organizations, groups and communities are increasingly connected as technology becomes more pervasive. As a result, there are many risks which present themselves including but not limited to malware, phishing, identity theft and broader threats to data security. It is essential that users and organizations are able recognize, manage and prevent these risks.

What is Digital Resilience?

Digital resilience supports individuals to recognize and manage the risks they come across when they socialize, explore or work online. It is achieved primarily through experience; trainings led by industry experts and can be reinforced through strong institutional policies and technical infrastructure.

For individual users this means that they have the confidence in understanding their online presence and how they use digital platforms as well as being alert to potential risks and threats that they could face and actively mitigating them.

For organizations, in addition to the above, it is ensuring that they can rapidly adapt to business disruptions by leveraging digital capabilities to not only restore business operations, but also capitalize on the changed conditions.
