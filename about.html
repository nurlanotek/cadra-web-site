ABOUT US

University of Central Asia (UCA) is a private, not-for-profit, secular university, and was founded in 2000 through an International Treaty signed by the Presidents of Tajikistan, the Kyrgyz Republic, and Kazakhstan, and His Highness the Aga Khan, ratified by their respective parliaments and registered with the United Nations.

The mission of UCA is to promote the social and economic development of Central Asia, particularly its mountain communities, by offering an internationally recognized standard of higher education and enabling the peoples of the region to preserve their rich cultural heritage as assets for the future.

UCA has engaged in research since its inception in the following areas: economic and social policy, natural resource management, natural hazards and climate change, civil society development in Kyrgyz Republic, preservation, and promotion of the cultural heritage of Central Asia.

UCA's undergraduate program extends across two campuses in Naryn, Kyrgyz Republic, and Khorog, Tajikistan, near the Tien Shan and Pamir mountains. In response to the needs of rural mountain communities, UCA has established its two campuses away from major urban centers. They are on the historic Silk Road, and UCA hopes to be at the heart of the economic and intellectual transformation taking place in the region. UCA offers four undergraduate disciplines: Communications and Media, Computer Science, Global Economics as well as Earth and Environmental Sciences.

Computer Science Department at UCA combines the mathematical building blocks of theoretical knowledge with applied programming skills. The concepts of hardware and computer architecture are provided in addition to advanced software topics to create a comprehensive preparation for students to enter the world of technology, start their own business, or pursue study at a higher level. Alongside Computer Science coursework, practical projects are assigned in various courses to provide the capability of dealing with unanticipated problems and to share their findings in refereed journals. The programme, taught by experienced and renowned faculty, will equip students to be part of the region’s new generation of tech specialists who will develop infrastructure, generate entrepreneurial opportunities, energize the economy and create a new vision for the future.

Learn more about UCA at ucentralasia.org

OUR MISSION

The aim of this platform is to provide support to public as well as private entities and civil society organizations in building their digital resilience through training programs, knowledge dissemination, and cyber policy discussions of international standards to make Kyrgyz Republic a leader in digital resilience and cyber security.

Notable Accomplishments:
  • In 2019, University of Central Asia, in collaboration with the IT Department of the State Committee for Information Technology and Communications, and the High Technology Park of the Kyrgyz Republic organized a DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION IN CENTRAL ASIA CONFERENCE as part of the Year of Regional Development and Country Digitization declared by the President of the Kyrgyz Republic. The conference has been a springboard for galvanizing a strategy for digital transformation within the Kyrgyz Republic. Professionals, academics, business leaders and government officials explored the technological trends facing Central Asia and more specifically the Kyrgyz Republic. The conference sought advice from experienced professionals and reflected on the risks and opportunities technology is likely to bring in the future. For more information, see: https://dtca.kg/
  • In 2021, University of Central Asia received a grant from the Korea World Bank Partnership Facility (KWPF) to deliver “Digital Resilience Leadership Training Program” - a series of courses for governmental officials, private sector representatives and civil society organizations to strengthen their capacity to understand, identify, monitor, and respond effectively to cyber threats.
  • On April, 2022, we have successfully organized digital resilience and cyber security trainings to fifty government officials from all ministries of Kyrgyz Republic.
